Impact On Enviroment

Semiconductor manufacturing has a significant environmental impact due to high water usage, energy consumption, chemical waste, and electronic waste. This can strain water supplies, increase greenhouse gas emissions, and release hazardous materials into the environment. However, efforts are being made to mitigate these impacts through the adoption of sustainable practices and cleaner production processes.

Efforts to reduce water usage, energy consumption, and hazardous chemical disposal, while promoting responsible recycling of electronic waste, are necessary to reduce the environmental impact of semiconductor manufacturing.

A method currently being employed involves the installation of water recycling systems that purify and reuse water, resulting in a decrease in the amount of fresh water required for manufacturing processes. Additionally, the integration of renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power can aid in reducing the carbon footprint associated with semiconductor manufacturing.
