Photos that explain how manufacturing effect the environment:

Semiconductor manufacturing produces electronic components and devices, contributing to electronic waste when they become obsolete, resulting in an increase in e-waste generation.

The water-intensive processes in semiconductor manufacturing can cause chemical discharges, leading to water pollution. Thus, semiconductor manufacturing is linked to water pollution.

The energy-intensive processes in semiconductor manufacturing lead to energy pollution from both fossil fuels and renewables. The production and disposal of semiconductor devices also contribute to energy pollution.

The use of a variety of chemicals in semiconductor manufacturing can lead to chemical pollution if not handled or disposed of correctly. Hence, semiconductor manufacturing is linked to chemical pollution.

The energy-intensive processes in semiconductor manufacturing lead to greenhouse gas emissions from fossil fuels. The production and disposal of semiconductor devices also contribute to greenhouse gas emissions.
